# Unit 1: Chemistry — Chemical Processes

## 1. Atomic Structure (원자 구조)

An **atom (원자)** is the smallest unit of an element that still has the properties of that element.

Atoms contain:

| Particle | Charge | Location | Korean |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Proton | +1 | Nucleus | 양성자 |
| Neutron | 0 | Nucleus | 중성자 |
| Electron | -1 | Energy levels/shells | 전자 |

Important rules:

- **Atomic number (원자 번호)** = number of protons
- In a neutral atom, protons = electrons
- **Mass number (질량수)** = protons + neutrons
- **Neutrons (중성자)** = mass number - atomic number
- **Valence electrons (원자가 전자)** = electrons in the outermost shell

Example:

```text
Sodium (Na)
Atomic number = 11
Protons = 11
Electrons = 11
Mass number ≈ 23
Neutrons = 23 - 11 = 12
```

---

## 2. Ions (이온)

An **ion (이온)** is an atom or group of atoms with a charge.

Atoms become ions by gaining or losing electrons.

### Cation (양이온)

A **cation (양이온)** is a positive ion.

It forms when an atom **loses electrons**.

Example:

```text
Na → Na⁺ + e⁻
```

Sodium loses one electron and becomes Na⁺.

### Anion (음이온)

An **anion (음이온)** is a negative ion.

It forms when an atom **gains electrons**.

Example:

```text
Cl + e⁻ → Cl⁻
```

Chlorine gains one electron and becomes Cl⁻.

---

## 3. Bohr-Rutherford Diagrams (보어-러더퍼드 도식)

A **Bohr-Rutherford diagram (보어-러더퍼드 도식)** shows:

1. The nucleus with protons and neutrons
2. Electron shells
3. Electrons arranged in shells

For SNC2D, shell capacity is usually:

```text
1st shell: 2 electrons
2nd shell: 8 electrons
3rd shell: 8 electrons for simple diagrams
```

Example: Oxygen

```text
Oxygen atomic number = 8
Electrons = 8
Arrangement = 2, 6
Valence electrons = 6
```

Oxygen tends to gain 2 electrons to form O²⁻.

---

## 4. Ionic Compounds vs Molecular Compounds

## Ionic Compounds (이온 화합물)

An **ionic compound (이온 화합물)** forms between a **metal (금속)** and a **non-metal (비금속)**.

Key idea:

> Electrons are transferred.

Properties:

- Usually high melting point
- Often soluble in water
- Conduct electricity when dissolved or molten
- Made of ions
- Metal becomes positive, non-metal becomes negative

Examples:

```text
NaCl
MgO
CaCl₂
KBr
Fe₂O₃
```

## Molecular Compounds / Covalent Compounds (분자 화합물 / 공유 화합물)

A **molecular compound (분자 화합물)** forms between **non-metals (비금속)**.

Key idea:

> Electrons are shared.

Properties:

- Usually lower melting point than ionic compounds
- Poor electrical conductivity
- Made of molecules
- Often gases or liquids at room temperature, though not always

Examples:

```text
CO₂
H₂O
NO₂
CCl₄
N₂O₅
```

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Ionic Compound (이온 화합물) | Molecular Compound (분자 화합물) |
|---|---|---|
| Elements | Metal + non-metal | Non-metal + non-metal |
| Bond | Electron transfer | Electron sharing |
| Particles | Ions | Molecules |
| Conductivity | Good when dissolved/molten | Usually poor |
| Melting point | Usually high | Usually lower |

---

## 5. Ionic Bonding (이온 결합)

An **ionic bond (이온 결합)** is the attraction between oppositely charged ions.

Example: sodium chloride

```text
Na loses 1 electron → Na⁺
Cl gains 1 electron → Cl⁻
Na⁺ and Cl⁻ attract → NaCl
```

The compound is neutral overall because the total positive charge equals the total negative charge.

---

## 6. Covalent Bonding (공유 결합)

A **covalent bond (공유 결합)** forms when atoms share electrons.

Example: water

```text
Oxygen shares electrons with two hydrogen atoms.
Formula: H₂O
```

Example: carbon dioxide

```text
Carbon shares electrons with two oxygen atoms.
Formula: CO₂
```

In **Lewis diagrams (루이스 구조)**:

- Dots show valence electrons
- Lines show shared electron pairs
- Lone pairs should be shown when needed

---

## 7. Writing Ionic Formulas: Criss-Cross Method (크리스크로스 방법)

The **criss-cross method (크리스크로스 방법)** helps write formulas for ionic compounds.

Steps:

1. Write the ions with charges
2. Cross the charge numbers down
3. Remove charge signs
4. Reduce if possible
5. Use brackets for multiple polyatomic ions

Example: aluminum chloride

```text
Al³⁺ and Cl⁻
Criss-cross → AlCl₃
```

Example: calcium carbonate

```text
Ca²⁺ and CO₃²⁻
Criss-cross → Ca₂(CO₃)₂
Reduce → CaCO₃
```

Example: barium hydroxide

```text
Ba²⁺ and OH⁻
Criss-cross → Ba(OH)₂
```

## 8. Common Polyatomic Ions (다원자 이온)

A **polyatomic ion (다원자 이온)** is a charged group of atoms that stays together.

| Ion | Name | Korean |
|---|---|---|
| NH₄⁺ | ammonium | 암모늄 |
| OH⁻ | hydroxide | 수산화 이온 |
| NO₃⁻ | nitrate | 질산 이온 |
| NO₂⁻ | nitrite | 아질산 이온 |
| CO₃²⁻ | carbonate | 탄산 이온 |
| HCO₃⁻ | hydrogen carbonate / bicarbonate | 탄산수소 이온 |
| SO₄²⁻ | sulfate | 황산 이온 |
| SO₃²⁻ | sulfite | 아황산 이온 |
| PO₄³⁻ | phosphate | 인산 이온 |
| PO₃³⁻ | phosphite | 아인산 이온 |

Important:

> Treat a polyatomic ion as one unit. Use brackets if there is more than one.

Example:

```text
Al³⁺ + SO₄²⁻ → Al₂(SO₄)₃
```

## 9. Naming Ionic Compounds (이온 화합물 이름)

### Binary Ionic Compounds

Formula:

```text
metal name + non-metal ending in -ide
```

Examples:

```text
NaCl = sodium chloride
MgO = magnesium oxide
CaF₂ = calcium fluoride
K₂S = potassium sulfide
```

### Ionic Compounds with Polyatomic Ions

Keep the polyatomic ion name.

Examples:

```text
NaOH = sodium hydroxide
CaCO₃ = calcium carbonate
Ba(NO₃)₂ = barium nitrate
Al₂(SO₄)₃ = aluminum sulfate
```

### Ionic Compounds with Multivalent Metals (다가 금속)

Use Roman numerals to show the charge of the metal.

Examples:

```text
FeCl₂ = iron(II) chloride
FeCl₃ = iron(III) chloride
Cu₂O = copper(I) oxide
CuO = copper(II) oxide
```

How to find the Roman numeral:

```text
FeCl₂:
Each Cl = -1
Two Cl = -2 total
Fe must be +2
Name = iron(II) chloride
```

## 10. Naming Molecular Compounds (분자 화합물 이름)

Use prefixes.

| Number | Prefix |
|---:|---|
| 1 | mono- |
| 2 | di- |
| 3 | tri- |
| 4 | tetra- |
| 5 | penta- |
| 6 | hexa- |
| 7 | hepta- |
| 8 | octa- |
| 9 | nona- |
| 10 | deca- |

Rules:

1. First element keeps its name.
2. Second element ends in `-ide`.
3. Use prefixes to show number of atoms.
4. Usually do not use `mono-` on the first element.

Examples:

```text
CO₂ = carbon dioxide
CO = carbon monoxide
N₂O₅ = dinitrogen pentoxide
CCl₄ = carbon tetrachloride
P₂O₅ = diphosphorus pentoxide
SO₃ = sulfur trioxide
```

## 11. Physical Change vs Chemical Change

A **physical change (물리 변화)** changes form, shape, or state, but no new substance is made.

Examples:

```text
melting ice
boiling water
cutting paper
dissolving sugar
```

A **chemical change (화학 변화)** produces a new substance.

Five clues of chemical change:

1. **New colour (색 변화)**
2. **Gas formation / bubbles (기체 생성)**
3. **Precipitate formation (침전물 생성)**
4. **Light produced (빛 생성)**
5. **Temperature change (온도 변화)** without external heating/cooling

## 12. Chemical Equations (화학 반응식)

### Word Equation (단어 반응식)

Uses words.

```text
hydrogen + oxygen → water
```

### Skeleton Equation (골격 반응식)

Uses formulas but may not be balanced.

```text
H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
```

### Balanced Chemical Equation (균형 화학 반응식)

Has the same number of each atom on both sides.

```text
2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
```

---

## 13. Law of Conservation of Mass (질량 보존 법칙)

The **law of conservation of mass (질량 보존 법칙)** states:

> Matter cannot be created or destroyed in a chemical reaction.

Therefore:

```text
Mass of reactants = mass of products
```

This is why equations must be balanced.

Example:

```text
Unbalanced: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
Balanced:   2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
```

---

## 14. Types of Chemical Reactions (화학 반응 종류)

## A. Synthesis Reaction (합성 반응)

Two or more reactants combine to form one product.

General form:

```text
A + B → AB
```

Examples:

```text
2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl
2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃
```

---

## B. Decomposition Reaction (분해 반응)

One compound breaks into simpler substances.

General form:

```text
AB → A + B
```

Examples:

```text
2KClO₃ → 2KCl + 3O₂
2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
```

---

## C. Single Displacement Reaction (단일 치환 반응)

One element replaces another element in a compound.

General form:

```text
A + BC → AC + B
```

Examples:

```text
Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂
2Al + 3CuSO₄ → 3Cu + Al₂(SO₄)₃
```

---

## D. Double Displacement Reaction (이중 치환 반응)

Ions in two compounds switch partners.

General form:

```text
AB + CD → AD + CB
```

Examples:

```text
CuSO₄ + 2NaOH → Cu(OH)₂ + Na₂SO₄
Pb(NO₃)₂ + 2KI → PbI₂ + 2KNO₃
```

A **precipitate (침전물)** may form.

---

## E. Combustion Reaction (연소 반응)

A hydrocarbon reacts with oxygen.

Complete combustion produces carbon dioxide and water.

General form:

```text
hydrocarbon + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
```

Example:

```text
2C₈H₁₈ + 25O₂ → 16CO₂ + 18H₂O
```

---

## F. Neutralization Reaction (중화 반응)

An acid reacts with a base.

General form:

```text
acid + base → salt + water
```

Example:

```text
HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
```

---

## 15. Acids, Bases, and pH

## Acid (산)

An **acid (산)** has pH less than 7.

Properties:

- Sour
- pH < 7
- Corrosive
- Turns blue litmus red
- Reacts with metals to produce hydrogen gas

Examples:

```text
HCl = hydrochloric acid
HNO₃ = nitric acid
H₂SO₄ = sulfuric acid
CH₃COOH = acetic acid
```

## Base (염기)

A **base (염기)** has pH greater than 7.

Properties:

- Bitter
- Slippery
- pH > 7
- Corrosive
- Turns red litmus blue

Examples:

```text
NaOH = sodium hydroxide
Ca(OH)₂ = calcium hydroxide
NH₄OH = ammonium hydroxide
```

## pH Scale (pH 척도)

The **pH scale (pH 척도)** measures hydrogen ion concentration.

```text
pH < 7 → acidic
pH = 7 → neutral
pH > 7 → basic
```

The pH scale is logarithmic.

A difference of 1 pH unit means a 10 times difference.

Example:

```text
pH 10.8 is 10 times more basic than pH 9.8.
```

---

## 16. Chemical Indicator (지시약)

A **chemical indicator (지시약)** changes colour depending on whether a solution is acidic or basic.

Examples:

- Litmus paper
- Bromothymol blue
- Phenolphthalein
- Universal indicator

# Unit 2: Biology — Tissues, Organs, and Systems

## 1. Cell Theory (세포설)

The **cell theory (세포설)** has three main ideas:

1. All living things are made of one or more cells.
2. The cell is the basic unit of life.
3. All cells come from pre-existing cells.

---

|---|---|---|
| Nucleus | 핵 | Controls cell activities and contains DNA |
| Nuclear membrane | 핵막 | Surrounds and protects the nucleus |

## 3. Plant Cells vs Animal Cells

## Plant Cell (식물세포)

Plant cells have:

- **Cell wall (세포벽)**
- **Chloroplasts (엽록체)**
- Large central **vacuole (액포)**

## Animal Cell (동물세포)

Animal cells do not have:

- Cell wall
- Chloroplasts

## Mitosis Difference

During cytokinesis:

- Animal cells form a **cleavage furrow (분열구)**.
- Plant cells form a **cell plate (세포판)**.

---

## 4. Cell Transport (세포 수송)

## Passive Transport (수동 수송)

Passive transport does not require energy.

Examples:

- **Diffusion (확산)**
- **Osmosis (삼투)**

## Active Transport (능동 수송)

Active transport requires energy.

It moves substances against the concentration gradient.

```text
Low concentration → high concentration
```

---

## 5. Cell Cycle (세포 주기)

The **cell cycle (세포 주기)** includes:

1. **Interphase (간기)**
2. **Mitosis (유사분열)**
3. **Cytokinesis (세포질 분열)**

## Interphase (간기)

Interphase is usually the longest phase.

During interphase, the cell:

- Grows
- Performs normal functions
- Repairs itself
- Copies DNA
- Prepares for cell division

## Mitosis (유사분열)

Mitosis is nuclear division.

It produces two genetically identical nuclei.

## Cytokinesis (세포질 분열)

Cytokinesis divides the cytoplasm.

It produces two genetically identical daughter cells.

---

## 6. Stages of Mitosis (유사분열 단계)

Remember:

```text
PMAT
Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase
```

## Prophase (전기)

- Chromosomes condense and become visible.
- Nuclear membrane breaks down.
- Spindle fibres form.

## Metaphase (중기)

- Chromosomes line up in the middle of the cell.

## Anaphase (후기)

- Sister chromatids separate.
- Chromatids move to opposite poles.

## Telophase (말기)

- Nuclear membranes reform.
- Chromosomes begin to uncoil.

## Cytokinesis (세포질 분열)

- Cytoplasm divides.
- Two identical daughter cells form.

## 7. DNA, Chromosomes, Genes, and Proteins

## DNA (DNA)

**DNA (DNA)** is the genetic instruction molecule.

## Gene (유전자)

A **gene (유전자)** is a section of DNA that codes for a protein.

## Chromosome (염색체)

A **chromosome (염색체)** is a long structure made of DNA and proteins.

## Protein (단백질)

A **protein (단백질)** is made according to DNA instructions and performs many cell functions.

Connection:

```text
DNA contains genes.
Genes code for proteins.
DNA is organized into chromosomes.
Proteins help determine cell structure and function.
```

---

## 8. Mutations (돌연변이)

A **mutation (돌연변이)** is a change in DNA.

Causes:

- Random copying errors
- Radiation
- Chemicals
- Mutagens

A **mutagen (돌연변이 유발 물질)** is anything that increases the chance of mutation.

Possible effects:

- No effect
- Harmful effect
- Beneficial effect
- Cancer if cell division control is affected

---

## 9. Cell Specialization (세포 특수화)

**Cell specialization (세포 특수화)** means cells develop specific structures and functions.

Examples:

- Red blood cells carry oxygen.
- Muscle cells contract.
- Nerve cells send electrical messages.
- Palisade mesophyll cells perform photosynthesis.

## Cell Differentiation (세포 분화)

**Cell differentiation (세포 분화)** is the process where unspecialized cells become specialized.

---

## 10. Stem Cells (줄기세포)

A **stem cell (줄기세포)** is an unspecialized cell that can become different types of specialized cells.

## Totipotent Stem Cell (전능성 줄기세포)

Can become all cell types.

## Pluripotent Stem Cell (다능성 줄기세포)

Can become many types of cells, but not all.

Stem cells are important for:

- Growth
- Repair
- Regeneration
- Medical treatment research

## 15. Xylem and Phloem

## Xylem (물관)

Transports water and minerals.

Direction:

```text
roots → stem → leaves
```

## Phloem (체관)

Transports sugars and nutrients throughout the plant.

Direction depends on plant needs.

```text
source cell → sink cell
```

## 16. Leaf Structures (잎 구조)

A leaf is specialized for photosynthesis.

## Waxy Cuticle (큐티클층)

- Transparent so light can pass through
- Prevents water loss
- Protects the leaf

## Upper Epidermis (위쪽 표피)

- Protective layer
- Allows sunlight to enter

## Palisade Mesophyll (책상 조직)

- Main photosynthesis layer
- Cells are tightly packed
- Contains many chloroplasts

## Spongy Mesophyll (해면 조직)

- Has air spaces
- Allows gas movement
- Helps hold CO₂ for photosynthesis

## Stomata (기공)

Small openings for gas exchange.

```text
CO₂ enters
O₂ exits
Water vapour exits
```

## Guard Cells (공변세포)

Open and close stomata.

## Vascular Bundle (관다발)

Contains xylem and phloem.

```text
Xylem brings water.
Phloem carries sugars away.
```

## Fibrous Roots (수염뿌리)

- Shallow
- Many thin roots
- Large surface area
- Good for absorbing water near the surface

## Taproot (곧은뿌리)

- One main deep root
- Can store nutrients
- Can reach deeper water

## 18. Water Movement in Plants (식물의 물 이동)

Water moves from soil to leaves using several processes.

## Osmosis (삼투)

Water enters root cells by osmosis.

## Root Pressure (뿌리압)

Water entering roots creates pressure that pushes water upward.

## Adhesion (부착력)

Water molecules stick to xylem walls.

## Cohesion (응집력)

Water molecules stick to each other.

## Capillary Action (모세관 현상)

Adhesion and cohesion help water move through narrow xylem tubes.

## Transpiration (증산 작용)

Water evaporates from leaves, pulling more water upward.

Full explanation:

```text
Water enters root hairs by osmosis.
Root pressure pushes water upward.
Adhesion helps water stick to xylem walls.
Cohesion keeps water molecules connected.
Transpiration pulls water upward as water evaporates from leaves.
```

# Unit 3: Physics — Light and Geometric Optics

## 1. Nature of Light (빛의 성질)

**Light (빛)** is a form of electromagnetic radiation.

Properties:

- Travels in straight lines
- Travels at `3.00 × 10⁸ m/s` in a vacuum
- Does not require a medium
- Has wave-like properties
- Can reflect, refract, and disperse

---

## 2. Electromagnetic Spectrum (전자기파 스펙트럼)

The **electromagnetic spectrum (전자기파 스펙트럼)** is the full range of electromagnetic waves.

Order from lowest energy to highest energy:

```text
radio waves → microwaves → infrared → visible light → ultraviolet → X-rays → gamma rays
```

Order from longest wavelength to shortest wavelength:

```text
radio waves → microwaves → infrared → visible light → ultraviolet → X-rays → gamma rays
```

Order from shortest wavelength to longest wavelength:

```text
gamma rays → X-rays → ultraviolet → visible light → infrared → microwaves → radio waves
```

Visible light order:

```text
red → orange → yellow → green → blue → indigo → violet
```

Red has longer wavelength and lower energy.  
Violet has shorter wavelength and higher energy.

---

## 3. Sources of Light (빛의 발생 방식)

| Type | Korean | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incandescence | 백열 | Light from heating | Incandescent bulb, Sun |
| Electric discharge | 전기 방전 | Electricity passes through gas | Neon sign |
| Fluorescence | 형광 | Absorbs energy and immediately emits light | CFL bulb |
| Phosphorescence | 인광 | Emits light after energy source is removed | Glow-in-the-dark object |
| Chemiluminescence | 화학 발광 | Light from chemical reaction | Glow stick |
| Bioluminescence | 생물 발광 | Light from living organisms | Firefly, angler fish |

Fluorescence vs phosphorescence:

```text
Fluorescence stops almost immediately when the energy source is removed.
Phosphorescence continues glowing after the energy source is removed.
```

---

## 4. Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque

## Transparent (투명)

Light passes through clearly.

Example:

```text
clear glass
```

## Translucent (반투명)

Some light passes through, but the image is blurry.

Example:

```text
frosted glass
```

## Opaque (불투명)

Light does not pass through.

Example:

```text
wood, metal
```

---

## 5. Reflection (반사)

**Reflection (반사)** occurs when light bounces off a surface.

Important terms:

| Term | Korean | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Incident ray | 입사광선 | Incoming ray |
| Reflected ray | 반사광선 | Outgoing ray after reflection |
| Normal | 법선 | Perpendicular line to the surface |
| Angle of incidence | 입사각 | Angle between incident ray and normal |
| Angle of reflection | 반사각 | Angle between reflected ray and normal |

---

## 6. Law of Reflection (반사의 법칙)

The **law of reflection (반사의 법칙)** has two parts:

1. The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.
2. The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.

Formula:

```text
θi = θr
```

---

## 7. Specular and Diffuse Reflection

## Specular Reflection (정반사)

Occurs on smooth surfaces.

Produces a clear image.

Example:

```text
mirror
```

## Diffuse Reflection (난반사)

Occurs on rough surfaces.

Does not produce a clear image.

Example:

```text
paper, wall
```

## 8. SALT / LOST Image Descriptions

## SALT

| Letter | Meaning | Korean |
|---|---|---|

Possible answers:


## 9. Real Image vs Virtual Image

## Real Image (실상)

A real image forms where light rays actually meet.

Features:

- Can be projected on a screen
- Usually inverted
- Forms in front of mirrors or opposite side of lenses depending on setup

## Virtual Image (허상)

A virtual image forms where rays only appear to come from.

Features:

- Cannot be projected on a screen
- Usually upright
- Found by extending rays backward with dotted lines

---

## 10. Plane Mirror (평면거울)

A **plane mirror (평면거울)** always forms an image with these characteristics:

| Feature | Plane Mirror Image |
|---|---|
| Size | Same size |
| Attitude/Orientation | Upright |
| Location | Same distance behind mirror |
| Type | Virtual |

SALT:

```text
S: same size
A: upright
L: same distance behind the mirror
T: virtual
```

---

## 11. Curved Mirrors (곡면거울)

## Concave Mirror (오목거울)

A **concave mirror (오목거울)** curves inward.

It is a **converging mirror (수렴 거울)**.

Parallel rays reflect and meet at the focus.

Image depends on object location.

General rules:

| Object Location | Image |
|---|---|
| Beyond C | Smaller, inverted, real, between C and F |
| At C | Same size, inverted, real, at C |
| Between C and F | Larger, inverted, real, beyond C |
| At F | No image / image at infinity |
| Inside F | Larger, upright, virtual, behind mirror |

## Convex Mirror (볼록거울)

A **convex mirror (볼록거울)** curves outward.

It is a **diverging mirror (발산 거울)**.

Convex mirrors always produce:

```text
smaller + upright + virtual image
```

Examples:

- Store security mirrors
- Car side mirrors

---

## 12. Mirror Equation and Magnification

Mirror equation:

```math
\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}
```

Magnification equation:

```math
M = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}
```

Variables:

| Symbol | Meaning | Korean |
|---|---|---|
| `f` | focal length | 초점거리 |
| `d_o` | object distance | 물체 거리 |
| `d_i` | image distance | 상 거리 |
| `h_o` | object height | 물체 높이 |
| `h_i` | image height | 상 높이 |
| `M` | magnification | 배율 |

Sign meaning:

```text
Positive d_i → real image
Negative d_i → virtual image
Positive h_i → upright image
Negative h_i → inverted image
```

---

## 13. Refraction (굴절)

**Refraction (굴절)** occurs when light changes direction because it changes speed when entering a different medium.

A **medium (매질)** is a material that light travels through.

Examples:

```text
air, water, glass, diamond
```

---

## 14. Index of Refraction (굴절률)

The **index of refraction (굴절률)** shows how much a medium slows down light.

Formula:

```math
n = \frac{c}{v}
```

Variables:

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `n` | index of refraction |
| `c` | speed of light in vacuum = `3.00 × 10⁸ m/s` |
| `v` | speed of light in medium |

Higher `n` means:

```text
Light travels slower.
The medium is more optically dense.
Light bends more.
```

---

## 15. Refraction Rules

When light travels from a faster medium to a slower medium:

```text
It bends toward the normal.
```

Example:

```text
air → glass
```

When light travels from a slower medium to a faster medium:

```text
It bends away from the normal.
```

Example:

```text
water → air
```

High `n` = slower medium.  
Low `n` = faster medium.

---

## 16. Optical Density (광학적 밀도)

**Optical density (광학적 밀도)** means how much a material slows down light.

More optically dense:

```text
higher index of refraction
slower light speed
```

Less optically dense:

```text
lower index of refraction
faster light speed
```

---

## 17. Total Internal Reflection (전반사)

**Total internal reflection (전반사)** occurs when light reflects completely inside a medium instead of refracting out.

Conditions:

1. Light travels from higher `n` to lower `n`.
2. The angle of incidence is greater than the **critical angle (임계각)**.

Applications:

- Optical fibres
- Diamonds
- Binoculars
- Retroreflectors
- Some medical imaging technologies

---

## 18. Refraction Phenomena

## Apparent Depth (겉보기 깊이)

Objects underwater appear shallower than they really are because light refracts when moving from water to air.

## Dispersion (분산)

White light separates into colours because different wavelengths bend by different amounts.

Examples:

- Prism
- Rainbow

## Rainbow (무지개)

Caused by refraction, reflection, and dispersion in water droplets.

## Sundog / Parahelion (환일)

Bright spots near the Sun caused by ice crystals in the atmosphere.

## Mirage (신기루)

Caused by refraction through air layers at different temperatures.

## Shimmering (아지랑이)

Caused by light refracting through unevenly heated air.

---

## 19. Lenses (렌즈)

A **lens (렌즈)** forms images by refraction.

## Converging Lens (볼록렌즈 / 수렴 렌즈)

A converging lens is thicker in the middle.

It brings parallel light rays together at a focus.

Common uses:

- Magnifying glass
- Camera
- Correcting hyperopia

## Diverging Lens (오목렌즈 / 발산 렌즈)

A diverging lens is thinner in the middle.

It spreads parallel light rays apart.

Common uses:

- Correcting myopia

---

## 20. Thin Lens Equation

Thin lens equation:

```math
\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}
```

Magnification equation:

```math
M = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}
```

Important:

```text
Converging lens → positive focal length
Diverging lens → negative focal length
```

# Unit 4: Earth and Space Science — Climate Change

## 1. Weather vs Climate

## Weather (날씨)

Short-term atmospheric conditions.

Examples:

- Today’s temperature
- Rain tomorrow
- Wind speed now

## Climate (기후)

Long-term average weather patterns in a region.

Examples:

- Average yearly temperature
- Seasonal precipitation
- Typical wind patterns

---

## 2. Earth’s Energy Budget (지구 에너지 수지)

Earth maintains a relatively stable temperature because of a balance between:

```text
incoming solar radiation
and
outgoing thermal radiation
```

If incoming energy equals outgoing energy:

```text
Earth’s temperature stays stable.
```

If more energy enters than leaves:

```text
Earth warms.
```

If more energy leaves than enters:

```text
Earth cools.
```

---

## 3. Heat Transfer (열 전달)

There are three types of heat transfer.

## Radiation (복사)

Energy transfer by electromagnetic waves.

Important:

```text
Solar energy reaches Earth by radiation because radiation does not need a medium.
```

## Conduction (전도)

Heat transfer by direct contact.

Example:

```text
Warm ground heats air touching it.
```

## Convection (대류)

Heat transfer by movement of fluids.

Example:

```text
Warm air rises and cool air sinks.
```

---

## 4. Specific Heat Capacity (비열)

**Specific heat capacity (비열)** is the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of a substance.

Water has high specific heat capacity.

That means:

```text
Water heats slowly.
Water cools slowly.
Large lakes moderate nearby climate.
```

Land has lower specific heat capacity.

That means:

```text
Land heats quickly during the day.
Land cools quickly at night.
```

---

## 5. Water and Climate Moderation

Water helps moderate climate because:

1. It has high specific heat capacity.
2. It stores large amounts of thermal energy.
3. It moves energy through ocean currents.
4. It absorbs and releases energy during evaporation and condensation.

Coastal regions often have milder climates than inland regions.

---

## 6. Water Cycle (물 순환)

The **water cycle (물 순환)** moves water through Earth’s systems.

Main processes:

| Process | Korean | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Evaporation | 증발 | Liquid water becomes water vapour |
| Condensation | 응결 | Water vapour becomes liquid droplets |
| Precipitation | 강수 | Rain, snow, sleet, or hail falls |
| Runoff | 유출 | Water flows over land |
| Infiltration | 침투 | Water enters the ground |
| Transpiration | 증산 | Water vapour leaves plants |

---

## 7. Humidity (습도)

**Humidity (습도)** is the amount of water vapour in the air.

**Relative humidity (상대 습도)** is the percentage of water vapour in air compared with the maximum amount the air can hold at that temperature.

Example:

```text
Relative humidity = 62%
```

Meaning:

```text
The air contains 62% of the water vapour it could hold at that temperature.
```

When saturated moist air cools:

```text
Condensation occurs.
Clouds or precipitation may form.
```

---

## 8. Atmosphere Layers (대기층)

| Layer | Korean | Important Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Troposphere | 대류권 | Weather occurs here |
| Stratosphere | 성층권 | Contains ozone layer |
| Mesosphere | 중간권 | Meteors burn up |
| Thermosphere | 열권 | Auroras occur; very high temperature |

---

## 9. Atmospheric Pressure (기압)

**Atmospheric pressure (기압)** is caused by the weight of air above a location.

Important rules:

```text
Warm air is less dense and rises.
Cool air is denser and sinks.
```

High pressure often brings clear weather.  
Low pressure often brings clouds or storms.

---

## 10. Latitude and Solar Radiation

Earth is spherical, so sunlight hits different latitudes at different angles.

Near the equator:

```text
Sunlight is direct.
Energy is concentrated.
Climate is warmer.
```

Near the poles:

```text
Sunlight is less direct.
Energy spreads over a larger area.
Climate is colder.
```

---

## 11. Seasons (계절)

Seasons are mainly caused by Earth’s axial tilt.

Earth’s axis is tilted about 23.5°.

Canada is colder in winter because:

1. The Northern Hemisphere is tilted away from the Sun.
2. Sunlight hits at a lower angle.
3. Days are shorter.
4. Less solar energy is absorbed.

---

## 12. Atmospheric Circulation (대기 순환)

Warm air rises near the equator because sunlight is more direct there.

Basic pattern:

```text
Warm air rises.
Cool air sinks.
Air moves from high pressure to low pressure.
This creates wind.
```

If Earth did not rotate, winds would move more directly from poles to equator at the surface.

Because Earth rotates, winds curve.

---

## 13. Coriolis Effect (코리올리 효과)

The **Coriolis effect (코리올리 효과)** is the apparent deflection of moving air and water caused by Earth’s rotation.

Northern Hemisphere:

```text
Moving air/water curves to the right.
```

Southern Hemisphere:

```text
Moving air/water curves to the left.
```

This affects:

- Prevailing winds
- Ocean currents
- Storm rotation

---

## 14. Prevailing Winds and Jet Streams

## Prevailing Winds (우세풍)

Prevailing winds are common wind patterns in a region.

Major wind belts:

- **Trade winds (무역풍)**
- **Prevailing westerlies (편서풍)**
- **Polar easterlies (극동풍)**

## Jet Streams (제트기류)

Jet streams are fast-moving winds high in the atmosphere.

They form near boundaries between warm and cold air masses.

---

## 15. Sea Breeze (해풍)

A **sea breeze (해풍)** occurs during the day.

Steps:

```text
Land heats faster than water.
Air over land warms and rises.
Cooler air from the water moves toward land.
Wind blows from sea to land.
```

---

## 16. Ocean Currents (해류)

**Ocean currents (해류)** move thermal energy around Earth.

Surface currents are affected by:

1. Wind
2. Earth’s rotation / Coriolis effect
3. Shape of continents

Ocean currents affect coastal climates.

Example:

```text
The Gulf Stream carries warm water toward Europe, making some coastal regions warmer.
```

## 17. Gyres (환류)

A **gyre (환류)** is a large circular ocean current system.

Northern Hemisphere:

```text
Usually clockwise
```

Southern Hemisphere:

```text
Usually counterclockwise
```

This difference is caused by the Coriolis effect.

## 18. Salinity and Density

**Salinity (염도)** is the salt concentration of seawater.

Important rules:

```text
Saltier water is denser.
Colder water is denser.
Dense water sinks.
Less dense water rises.
```

This helps drive deep ocean circulation.

## 19. Upwelling (용승)

**Upwelling (용승)** occurs when deep, cold, nutrient-rich water rises to the surface.

Why it matters:

```text
It brings nutrients to the surface.
It supports marine life.
It can affect local climate and fisheries.
```

## 20. El Niño (엘니뇨)

**El Niño (엘니뇨)** is a climate pattern involving unusually warm surface water in the Pacific Ocean.

Signs of El Niño:

- Trade winds weaken
- Warm water spreads eastward
- Upwelling decreases near western South America
- Rainfall and storm patterns change

Effects:

- Some regions become wetter
- Some regions become drier
- Ocean ecosystems can be affected


## 22. Greenhouse Effect (온실 효과)

The **greenhouse effect (온실 효과)** is a natural process that keeps Earth warm.

Steps:

```text
1. Solar radiation enters the atmosphere.
2. Earth’s surface absorbs energy and warms.
3. Earth emits infrared radiation.
4. Greenhouse gases absorb some infrared radiation.
5. Energy is re-radiated back toward Earth.
```

Without the natural greenhouse effect, Earth would be much colder.

## 24. Anthropogenic Greenhouse Effect

The **anthropogenic greenhouse effect (인위적 온실 효과)** is the increased greenhouse effect caused by human-produced greenhouse gases.

Human activities that contribute:

- Burning fossil fuels
- Driving gasoline cars
- Industrial processes
- Deforestation
- Agriculture and livestock
- Waste production

- Volcanoes
- Livestock

## Sink (흡수원)

## 26. Albedo (알베도)

**Albedo (알베도)** is the fraction of sunlight reflected by a surface.

High albedo:

```text
reflects more sunlight
absorbs less energy
```

Examples:

- Snow
- Ice
- White roof

Low albedo:

```text
reflects less sunlight
absorbs more energy
```

Examples:

- Ocean
- Asphalt
- Forest
- Dark soil

---

## 27. Ice-Albedo Feedback (얼음-알베도 피드백)

This is a **positive feedback loop (양의 피드백)**.

Steps:

```text
1. Global temperature rises.
2. Ice melts.
3. Albedo decreases.
4. Earth absorbs more solar radiation.
5. Temperature rises more.
6. More ice melts.
```

# High-Yield Short Answer Templates

## Chemistry Templates

### Ionic vs Molecular

```text
Ionic compounds form between metals and non-metals by electron transfer. Molecular compounds form between non-metals by electron sharing. Ionic compounds usually have higher melting points and can conduct electricity when dissolved, while molecular compounds usually have lower melting points and poor conductivity.
```

### Law of Conservation of Mass

```text
The law of conservation of mass states that matter cannot be created or destroyed. Therefore, chemical equations must be balanced so that the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides.
```

### Neutralization

```text
A neutralization reaction occurs when an acid reacts with a base to produce salt and water.
```

### Chemical Change Evidence

```text
Evidence of a chemical change includes colour change, gas formation, precipitate formation, light production, and temperature change without an external heat source.
```

## Biology Templates

### Mitosis

```text
Mitosis is nuclear division that produces two genetically identical daughter cells. The stages are prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase, followed by cytokinesis.
```

### Cell Cycle

```text
The cell cycle includes interphase, mitosis, and cytokinesis. During interphase, the cell grows, performs normal functions, and copies its DNA. During mitosis, the nucleus divides. During cytokinesis, the cytoplasm divides.
```

### Water Movement in Plants

```text
Water enters root hairs by osmosis. Root pressure pushes water upward, while adhesion and cohesion help water move through xylem. Transpiration from leaves pulls water upward through the plant.
```

### Respiratory Pathway

```text
Air enters through the mouth or nose, passes through the pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi, and bronchioles, and reaches the alveoli, where gas exchange occurs.
```

### Digestive Pathway

```text
Food travels from the mouth to the pharynx, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum, and anus. Most digestion and absorption occur in the small intestine.
```

### Blood Pathway

```text
Deoxygenated blood enters the right atrium, moves to the right ventricle, travels through the pulmonary arteries to the lungs, returns oxygenated through the pulmonary veins to the left atrium, moves to the left ventricle, and exits through the aorta to the body.
```

## Physics Templates

### Law of Reflection

```text
The law of reflection states that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.
```

### Plane Mirror Image

```text
A plane mirror forms an image that is the same size, upright, the same distance behind the mirror as the object is in front, and virtual.
```

### Refraction

```text
Refraction occurs when light changes speed as it enters a different medium, causing it to bend. When light enters a slower, more optically dense medium, it bends toward the normal. When it enters a faster medium, it bends away from the normal.
```

### Total Internal Reflection

```text
Total internal reflection occurs when light travels from a medium with a higher index of refraction to a lower index of refraction and the angle of incidence is greater than the critical angle.
```

## Climate Templates

### Greenhouse Effect

```text
The greenhouse effect occurs when greenhouse gases absorb infrared radiation emitted by Earth and re-radiate some of it back toward the surface, warming the planet.
```

### Anthropogenic Greenhouse Effect

```text
The anthropogenic greenhouse effect is the increased greenhouse effect caused by human activities that release extra greenhouse gases, such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and agriculture.
```

### Albedo

```text
Albedo is the fraction of sunlight reflected by a surface. Snow and ice have high albedo because they reflect a lot of sunlight, while oceans and asphalt have low albedo because they absorb more sunlight.
```

### Ice-Albedo Feedback

```text
When ice melts, Earth’s albedo decreases. This causes more solar radiation to be absorbed, which increases warming and causes more ice to melt. This is a positive feedback loop.
```

### Coriolis Effect

```text
The Coriolis effect is the apparent deflection of moving air and water caused by Earth’s rotation. In the Northern Hemisphere, moving air and water deflect to the right; in the Southern Hemisphere, they deflect to the left.
```

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

## Chemistry

- Do not change subscripts when balancing equations.
- Use brackets for multiple polyatomic ions.
- Use Roman numerals for multivalent metals.
- Do not use prefixes for ionic compounds.
- Do not forget diatomic elements like H₂, O₂, N₂, Cl₂, F₂, Br₂, I₂.

## Biology

- Do not confuse mitosis with cytokinesis.
- Do not say veins always carry deoxygenated blood; pulmonary veins carry oxygenated blood.
- Do not say arteries always carry oxygenated blood; pulmonary arteries carry deoxygenated blood.
- Do not confuse bronchi with bronchioles.
- Do not forget that alveoli are the gas exchange site.
- Do not confuse xylem and phloem.

## Physics

- Angles are measured from the normal, not the surface.
- Higher index of refraction means slower light.
- Faster to slower medium means toward the normal.
- Slower to faster medium means away from the normal.
- Plane mirror images are always virtual.
- Convex mirror images are always smaller, upright, and virtual.

## Climate

- Weather is short-term; climate is long-term.
- Radiation does not need a medium.
- Conduction requires direct contact.
- Convection requires fluid movement.
- Albedo means reflection, not absorption.
- Coriolis effect is caused by Earth’s rotation.
